Top Springs / Groundwater Researchers at UF Water Institute
==========================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on Florida springs, groundwater systems,
and the Floridan Aquifer at the UF Water Institute, ranked by their topic-specific
research (grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. Wendy Graham - Agricultural and Biological Engineering
   Expertise: Groundwater resources, Floridan Aquifer, hydrologic modeling, water sustainability
   Key Work: Founding Director of UF Water Institute; Carl S. Swisher Eminent Scholar;
   $4.9M grant on "Agricultural Water Security through Sustainable Use of the Floridan Aquifer";
   research on nitrate leaching to groundwater in Suwannee River Basin
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=uqVzdCMAAAAJ&hl=en

2. Jonathan B. Martin - Geological Sciences
   Expertise: Karst aquifers, Floridan Aquifer system, groundwater-surface water interaction
   Key Work: Distinguished Professor; 8,000+ citations; research on nutrient and carbon
   fluxes through Florida's carbonate aquifer systems; submarine groundwater discharge;
   springs geochemistry and water-rock interactions
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=pRh2pjMAAAAJ&hl=en

3. Matthew J. Cohen - School of Forest, Fisheries, and Geomatics Sciences
   Expertise: Florida springs ecology, spring ecosystem degradation, nutrient contamination
   Key Work: Water Institute Director; pioneering research on ecological state of Florida's
   springs (Silver, Wakulla, Rainbow, Ichetucknee); uses sensor technologies to understand
   nutrient contamination in springs; $456K NSF grant on Santa Fe River springs
   Website: https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/cohen-matthew/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cFbUqrEAAAAJ&hl=en

4. Michael D. Annable - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: Groundwater remediation, contaminant transport, aquifer characterization
   Key Work: Department Head, Environmental Engineering Sciences; research on subsurface
   remediation technologies, passive flux meters, karst aquifer nitrate attenuation;
   5+ patents in hydrologic measurement technology
   Website: https://faculty.eng.ufl.edu/michael-annable/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=t8-AZaEAAAAJ&hl=en

5. James W. Jawitz - Soil, Water and Ecosystem Sciences
   Expertise: Aquifer systems, contaminant hydrology, water sustainability
   Key Work: Landscape Hydrology Lab; research on aquifer remediation and
   groundwater-surface water interactions; nitrate attenuation in karst systems
   Website: https://www.landscapehydrology.org/what-we-do/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=nW8_WI0AAAAJ&hl=en
