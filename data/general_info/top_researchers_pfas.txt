Top PFAS / Emerging Contaminants Researchers at UF Water Institute
====================================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS),
emerging contaminants, and environmental contamination at the UF Water Institute,
ranked by their topic-specific research (grants, publications, leadership).

---

1. John Bowden - Veterinary Medicine
   Expertise: Mass spectrometry, PFAS detection methods, statewide PFAS mapping
   Key Work: $899K EPA grant on PFAS in landfills; FluoroMatch 2.0 PFAS detection software;
   statewide PFAS surveillance in Florida surface water and drinking water
   Website: https://www.vetmed.ufl.edu/about-the-college/directories/faculty/john-bowden/
   Google Scholar: https://www.researchgate.net/profile/John_Bowden2

2. Katherine Deliz Quinones - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: PFAS fate and transport, community health impacts, coastal contamination
   Key Work: $798K EPA grant "Building Resilience to PFAS Exposure in Vulnerable Coastal Communities";
   Lead investigator on PFAS in Indian River Lagoon; PFAS in swimming pools research
   Website: https://essie.ufl.edu/people/name/katherine-deliz-quinones/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=t8vTtBQAAAAJ&hl=en

3. Tracie Baker - Environmental and Global Health
   Expertise: PFAS toxicology, transgenerational effects, endocrine disruption
   Key Work: $2.1M NIEHS grants on environmental contaminants; "Transgenerational Effects of PFAS
   in Zebrafish" (50 citations); Director of WATER (Warrior Aquatic, Translational, and
   Environmental Research) Laboratory
   Website: www.bakerWATERlab.com

4. Bridget Baker - Wildlife Ecology and Conservation
   Expertise: PFAS bioaccumulation in wildlife, aquatic animal health, One Health approaches
   Key Work: "Nonlethal detection of PFAS bioaccumulation in fish" (35 citations);
   "Novel PFAS identification in Great Lakes watershed"; Co-PI, UF Marine Animal Rescue Program
   Website: https://wec.ifas.ufl.edu/people/wec-faculty/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=ajVHP8MAAAAJ&hl=en

5. Nancy Denslow - Physiological Sciences
   Expertise: Toxicogenomics, contaminant biomarkers, endocrine disruption detection
   Key Work: Pioneer in molecular biomonitoring of water quality; "PFOS levels during
   Hurricane Dorian" (19 citations); develops early-warning detection methods for contaminants
   Website: https://toxicology.vetmed.ufl.edu/people/faculty__trashed/nancy-denslow-phd/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=rJ--jD0AAAAJ&hl=en
