Top Hydrology / Watershed Researchers at UF Water Institute
===========================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on hydrology, watershed science, and
ecohydrology at the UF Water Institute, ranked by their topic-specific research
(grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. Matthew J. Cohen - School of Forest, Fisheries, and Geomatics Sciences
   Expertise: Ecohydrology, watershed biogeochemistry, Florida springs, Everglades restoration
   Key Work: Director of UF Water Institute; Carl S. Swisher Chair in Water Resources;
   $22M+ in research funding; ranked #1 in Hydrology at Water Institute;
   leads research on Florida springs and polar hydrology
   Website: https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/cohen-matthew/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cFbUqrEAAAAJ&hl=en

2. David Kaplan - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: Watershed ecology, wetland hydrology, hydrologic modeling, ecosystem services
   Key Work: Director of H.T. Odum Center for Wetlands; leads Watershed Ecology Lab;
   research on groundwater-surface water coupling, Everglades phosphorus, red tide blooms
   Website: https://www.watershedecology.org/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KDAxey0AAAAJ&hl=en

3. James W. Jawitz - Soil, Water and Ecosystem Sciences
   Expertise: Landscape hydrology, water sustainability, contaminant transport, watershed modeling
   Key Work: Leads Landscape Hydrology Laboratory; research on urban water vulnerability,
   nitrate export patterns, catchment hydrology; ranked #2 in Hydrology
   Website: https://www.landscapehydrology.org/what-we-do/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=nW8_WI0AAAAJ&hl=en

4. Wendy Graham - Agricultural and Biological Engineering
   Expertise: Hydrologic modeling, groundwater resources, climate impacts on water
   Key Work: Founding Director of UF Water Institute; Carl S. Swisher Eminent Scholar;
   $4.9M grant on Agricultural Water Security and Floridan Aquifer;
   research on nitrate leaching in Suwannee River Basin
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=uqVzdCMAAAAJ&hl=en

5. Rafael Munoz-Carpena - Agricultural and Biological Engineering
   Expertise: Hydrologic and water quality modeling, vegetative filter strips, watershed nutrient export
   Key Work: Distinguished Professor; developer of VFSMOD model; research on Lake Okeechobee
   water quality; $1.4M NSF grant on socio-ecological resilience; ranked #3 in Hydrology
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=8-GYxFAAAAAJ&hl=en
