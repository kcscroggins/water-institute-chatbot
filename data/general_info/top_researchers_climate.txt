Top Climate Change / Adaptation Researchers at UF Water Institute
=================================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on climate change impacts, climate
adaptation, and climate-water interactions at the UF Water Institute, ranked
by their topic-specific research (grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. Jane Southworth - Geography
   Expertise: Land change science, climate-vegetation interactions, remote sensing, GeoAI
   Key Work: Professor and Department Chair; ranked #1 in Ecological Applications;
   research on global biome shifts and vegetation persistence under climate change;
   $870K NASA grant on climate variability impacts in Southern Africa
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=L7iAPHcAAAAJ&hl=en

2. David Kaplan - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: Climate impacts on water systems, wetland responses to climate change
   Key Work: Director of H.T. Odum Center for Wetlands; research linking nitrogen
   discharges and climate to red tide blooms; climate change impacts on
   watershed-lake systems (Kissimmee River-Lake Okeechobee)
   Website: https://www.watershedecology.org/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KDAxey0AAAAJ&hl=en

3. Gerrit Hoogenboom - Agricultural and Biological Engineering
   Expertise: Agricultural climate modeling, crop-climate interactions, decision support systems
   Key Work: Ranked #3 overall at Water Institute; develops climate-agriculture models
   and decision support tools for climate adaptation in farming systems

4. Brett Scheffers - Wildlife Ecology and Conservation
   Expertise: Climate change impacts on biodiversity, species range shifts
   Key Work: Research on how climate change affects wildlife distributions and
   ecosystem structure; ranked high in Biological Sciences
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=4HfsgFUAAAAJ&hl=en

5. Matthew J. Cohen - School of Forest, Fisheries, and Geomatics Sciences
   Expertise: Polar hydrology, climate-water feedbacks, forest-water relationships
   Key Work: Water Institute Director; research on changing Arctic and Antarctic
   stream functions under climate change; climate impacts on water resources
   Website: https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/cohen-matthew/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cFbUqrEAAAAJ&hl=en
