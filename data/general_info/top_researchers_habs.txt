Top Harmful Algal Bloom (HAB) Researchers at UF Water Institute
===============================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on harmful algal blooms, cyanobacteria,
and algal ecology at the UF Water Institute, ranked by their topic-specific
research (grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. Edward Phlips - Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences
   Expertise: Phytoplankton ecology, harmful algal blooms, eutrophication, water quality
   Key Work: Leading HAB researcher at UF; research on Indian River Lagoon bloom dynamics,
   Lake Okeechobee algal modeling (24 citations); $181K NSF RAPID grant on nutrient
   impacts from mining facility; decades of HAB monitoring and assessment work
   ResearchGate: https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Edward-Phlips

2. H. Dail Laughinghouse IV - Agronomy (Ft. Lauderdale REC)
   Expertise: Cyanobacteria systematics, algal remediation, HAB science, algal turf scrubbers
   Key Work: Associate Professor of Applied Phycology; Florida Sea Grant HAB Extension specialist;
   research on toxic benthic cyanobacteria, cyanotoxin atmospheric transport;
   develops algal-based remediation technologies
   Website: https://flrec.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/h-dail-laughinghouse/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=bMKssuwAAAAJ&hl=en

3. David Kaplan - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: Nutrient dynamics driving algal blooms, watershed-coastal connections
   Key Work: Research on nitrogen-enriched discharges intensifying red tide (Karenia brevis)
   blooms in southwest Florida (68 citations); links watershed management to HAB outcomes
   Website: https://www.watershedecology.org/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KDAxey0AAAAJ&hl=en

4. Mark Brenner - Geological Sciences
   Expertise: Limnology, paleolimnology, lake ecosystem dynamics, historical bloom patterns
   Key Work: Research on long-term lake ecosystem changes and historical nutrient
   enrichment; understanding past bloom conditions to inform current management

5. Nancy Denslow - Physiological Sciences
   Expertise: Cyanotoxin detection, toxicological impacts of algal blooms
   Key Work: Research on biological effects of cyanotoxins and contaminants;
   develops biomarker methods for detecting bloom-related health impacts
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=rJ--jD0AAAAJ&hl=en
