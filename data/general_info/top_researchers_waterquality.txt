Top Water Quality Researchers at UF Water Institute
===================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on water quality science, nutrient
dynamics, and water quality management at the UF Water Institute, ranked by
their topic-specific research (grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. Matthew J. Cohen - School of Forest, Fisheries, and Geomatics Sciences
   Expertise: Water quality in springs and rivers, nutrient contamination, biogeochemistry
   Key Work: Water Institute Director; ranked #1 in Hydrology; research on nutrient
   dynamics in Florida springs; global stream dissolved oxygen patterns;
   $428K NSF grant on stream solute patterns
   Website: https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/cohen-matthew/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cFbUqrEAAAAJ&hl=en

2. Nancy Denslow - Physiological Sciences
   Expertise: Contaminant biomarkers, water quality biomonitoring, toxicogenomics
   Key Work: Ranked #1 in Pollution and Contamination; pioneer in molecular biomonitoring
   of water quality; develops early-warning detection methods for pollutants;
   research on endocrine disrupting compounds in aquatic systems
   Website: https://toxicology.vetmed.ufl.edu/people/faculty__trashed/nancy-denslow-phd/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=rJ--jD0AAAAJ&hl=en

3. Edward Phlips - Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences
   Expertise: Water quality assessment, phytoplankton ecology, eutrophication
   Key Work: Leading expert on algal indicators of water quality; research on
   nutrient-phytoplankton relationships in Indian River Lagoon and Lake Okeechobee;
   long-term water quality monitoring
   ResearchGate: https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Edward-Phlips

4. Rafael Munoz-Carpena - Agricultural and Biological Engineering
   Expertise: Water quality modeling, nutrient transport, BMP effectiveness
   Key Work: Distinguished Professor; research on Lake Okeechobee water quality;
   developer of VFSMOD for evaluating water quality BMPs; ranked #3 in Hydrology
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=8-GYxFAAAAAJ&hl=en

5. Mark Clark - Soil and Water Sciences
   Expertise: Nutrient cycling, phosphorus dynamics, soil-water quality linkages
   Key Work: Research on nutrient retention in wetlands; phosphorus in agricultural
   ditches; EPA Methods for Evaluating Wetland Condition; BMAP nutrient strategies
   Website: https://soils.ifas.ufl.edu/people/faculty/mark-clark/
