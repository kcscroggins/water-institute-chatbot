UF Water Institute - Educational Programs and Opportunities

Education and Outreach Mission:
The UF Water Institute fosters, supports and synergizes innovative interdisciplinary water education programs. The Institute implements innovative interdisciplinary academic programs to train excellent students and provides state-of-the-art expert assistance and educational programs for external stakeholders.

Water Institute Graduate Fellows (WIGF) Program:

The Water Institute Graduate Fellows (WIGF) Program was established in 2011 and successfully revamped in 2024. The program supports faculty-graduate teams to conduct innovative interdisciplinary research in emerging areas of water science, including the social, natural, and engineering sciences.

Program Goals:
- Provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the multidimensional challenges to sustaining water resources
- Equip students with a broad range of interdisciplinary skills
- Promote the establishment of research teams with long-lasting research connections that result in development of externally sponsored research

Funding Structure:
The new funding strategy involves matching funds from the UF Office of Research, participating department Chairs and Deans, and faculty to support students' fellowships.

Recent Cohorts:
Active cohorts from 2017, 2019, and 2024 have worked on diverse interdisciplinary water research projects ranging from high-latitude hydrology to water reuse challenges.

Hydrologic Sciences Academic Concentration (HSAC):

The Water Institute supports the Hydrologic Sciences Academic Concentration, an interdisciplinary graduate program that provides comprehensive training in water sciences.

Travel Awards:

The Water Institute Travel Awards provide financial support to UF graduate students to present water-related research at national or international conferences. These awards help students share their research and build professional networks.

For more information and to apply, visit: https://waterinstitute.ufl.edu/student-travel-award/

Supporting Multiple Graduate Programs:

The Water Institute works with graduate students across various UF departments and programs including:
- Environmental Engineering Sciences
- Soil and Water Sciences
- Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences
- Geography
- Agricultural and Biological Engineering
- Wildlife Ecology and Conservation
- And other related programs

Interdisciplinary Training:
Students gain exposure to multiple disciplines and perspectives on water challenges, preparing them for careers in research, government agencies, consulting, non-profits, and academia.
