About the Institute:
The University of Florida Water Institute was established in May 2006 as a campus-wide interdisciplinary organization. The Institute brings together talent from throughout the University and builds internal and external partnerships to address urgent water research challenges.

How to Connect:

Research Collaborations:
The Institute welcomes collaboration opportunities with researchers from other institutions, government agencies, and organizations working on water challenges.

Student Opportunities:
Graduate students interested in water research can explore opportunities through the Water Institute Graduate Fellows Program, travel awards, and connections with faculty across multiple departments.

Stakeholder Engagement:
The Institute provides expert assistance and educational programs for external stakeholders including health professionals, utility companies, regulatory agencies, and community organizations.

Symposium Information:
For information about the biennial UF Water Institute Symposium, visit the symposium website or contact the Institute office.
