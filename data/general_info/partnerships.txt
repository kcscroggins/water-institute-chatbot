UF Water Institute - Partnerships and Collaborations

Partnership Philosophy:

The University of Florida Water Institute brings together talent from throughout the University and builds internal and external partnerships to address urgent water research challenges. The Institute engages and collaborates with different stakeholders – such as health professionals, utility companies, regulatory agencies, and community organizations – to address water-related challenges.

Internal UF Collaborations:

UF Entities:
- Florida Climate Institute
- UF/IFAS Nature Coast Biological Station
- UF research deans and college leaders
- Multiple academic departments across campus
- UF/IFAS Extension programs

The Water Institute serves as a coordinating hub that connects water expertise across the entire University of Florida campus, bringing together faculty from geophysical, chemical, biological and social sciences, engineering, law, and humanities.

Corporate and Industry Partnerships:

Duke Energy:
The UF Water Institute partnered with Duke Energy to host the 8th biennial UF Water Institute Symposium on February 22-23, 2022. This partnership demonstrates collaboration between academia and the energy sector on water challenges.

Utility Companies and Regulatory Agencies:
The Institute works with water utilities and regulatory agencies to provide expert assistance and address real-world water management challenges.

Fellowship and Education Partnerships:

TESI Environmental Leaders Fellowship:
Partnership supporting environmental leadership development for graduate students.

Water Scholars Program:
Collaborative effort with UF research deans and college leaders to support interdisciplinary water research and education.

Stakeholder Engagement:

External Stakeholder Collaboration:
The Institute provides state-of-the-art expert assistance and educational programs for external stakeholders, working with:
- Health professionals on water quality and public health issues
- Utility companies on water management challenges
- Regulatory agencies on policy and compliance matters
- Community organizations on local water concerns

Symposium Partnerships:
The biennial UF Water Institute Symposium brings together scientists, policymakers, educators, practitioners, and stakeholders to share advances in water science and foster new collaborations.

Research Collaboration Model:

The Water Institute promotes establishment of research teams with long-lasting research connections through programs like the Graduate Fellows Program. These teams often develop into externally sponsored research projects, creating sustained partnerships with funding agencies and research organizations.
