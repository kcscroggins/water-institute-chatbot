Top Everglades / Wetlands Researchers at UF Water Institute
===========================================================

These researchers are the leading experts on the Everglades, wetland ecosystems,
and wetland restoration at the UF Water Institute, ranked by their topic-specific
research (grants, publications, leadership roles).

---

1. David Kaplan - Environmental Engineering Sciences
   Expertise: Wetland ecohydrology, Everglades restoration, nutrient cycling in wetlands
   Key Work: Director of H.T. Odum Center for Wetlands; research on Everglades
   phosphorus management; wetland restoration and ecosystem services;
   groundwater-surface water coupling in wetland systems
   Website: https://www.watershedecology.org/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KDAxey0AAAAJ&hl=en

2. Matthew J. Cohen - School of Forest, Fisheries, and Geomatics Sciences
   Expertise: Everglades ridge-slough landscapes, wetland biogeochemistry, restoration
   Key Work: Water Institute Director; pioneering research on autogenic patterned
   landscapes in Everglades; "Remote detection of ecosystem degradation in Everglades"
   (Remote Sensing of Environment); leads Everglades restoration research
   Website: https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/faculty/cohen-matthew/
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cFbUqrEAAAAJ&hl=en

3. Konda R. Reddy - Soil and Water Sciences
   Expertise: Wetland biogeochemistry, nutrient cycling, carbon dynamics
   Key Work: Distinguished Professor; foundational figure in wetland biogeochemistry;
   $5M+ in wetland research grants including $770K NSF on methane pathways;
   $3.2M IGERT on Adaptive Management; author of seminal wetland biogeochemistry texts

4. Todd Osborne - Soil and Water Sciences
   Expertise: Wetland soils, blue carbon, treatment wetlands, mangrove biogeochemistry
   Key Work: Research on Stormwater Treatment Areas (STAs); "Blue Carbon Soil Stock
   in Northern Florida Wetlands"; nitrogen effects on mangrove soils (71 citations);
   $775K grant for marine science infrastructure
   Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Fc2jwNAAAAAJ&hl=en

5. Mark Clark - Soil and Water Sciences
   Expertise: Wetland biogeochemistry, phosphorus retention, nutrient cycling
   Key Work: Research on when wetlands retain vs. release phosphorus; EPA Methods
   for Evaluating Wetland Condition; living shorelines and coastal wetland restoration
   Website: https://soils.ifas.ufl.edu/people/faculty/mark-clark/
