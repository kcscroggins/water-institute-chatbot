Program Focus:
Marnie Ward is known for applied Extension programming focused on community-based environmental education, agricultural and natural resource stewardship, and public engagement. Her work centers on translating science into practical guidance for local audiences, supporting informed decision-making related to land use, resource management, and environmental practices. She employs design and delivery of workshops trainings and educational events, development of Extension materials including fact sheets presentations and demonstrations, one-on-one and group technical assistance, and collaboration with researchers agencies and community organizations. Program systems include Florida communities served through UF/IFAS Extension, local and regional agricultural and natural resource systems, and extension audiences including landowners producers and community members.

Program Activities:
- Extension fact sheets and educational resources delivered through UF/IFAS
- Community workshops and outreach programs
- Educational events supporting agricultural and environmental best practices
- Technical assistance for landowners and producers
- Impact measured through knowledge transfer participation and adoption of recommended practices

Extension Impact:
Ward's work supports effective environmental and agricultural policy by ensuring that science-based guidance reaches stakeholders in forms that are practical, accessible, and actionable. She operates at the critical interface between science and practice, helping ensure that research-based recommendations are understood and applied by communities and land managers. Her work influences education and outreach effectiveness, stakeholder understanding and engagement, and adoption of science-based practices.
