Research Focus:
Dr. Zotarelli is an internationally recognized horticultural scientist known for his work on irrigation management, nitrogen and water use efficiency, and soil-plant-water interactions in vegetable cropping systems, particularly on sandy soils. He is especially well known for linking irrigation scheduling, nutrient management, and soil physical processes to improve crop productivity while reducing nutrient losses and environmental impacts. He employs field experiments in commercial and research-scale cropping systems, soil moisture sensing and interpretation, nitrogen mass balance and leaching measurements, evapotranspiration modeling using FAO-56 Penman-Monteith, integration of irrigation technology with nutrient management, and applied modeling and decision-support tools. Research systems include vegetable cropping systems in Florida and subtropical regions, sandy and low-organic-matter soils, drip irrigation seepage irrigation and plasticulture systems, and comparative systems including tropical and subtropical agroecosystems.

Policy Relevance:
Zotarelli's research underpins irrigation and nutrient management guidelines that improve water-use efficiency, reduce nutrient losses, and support sustainable agricultural production. He provides actionable, field-tested science that connects hydrology, soil processes, and nutrient management, directly shaping how intensive cropping systems are managed in environmentally sensitive regions. His work influences irrigation scheduling and water-use policy, nutrient management standards and BMPs, and grower adoption of soil moisture sensing and decision-support tools.
