Research Focus:
Dr. Munson is a marketing and consumer behavior researcher known for work on digital marketing, consumer privacy, advertising effects, and online decision-making. His research explores how information and technology shape consumer attitudes and behavior in digital environments. His work contributes to understanding digital consumer behavior, advertising effectiveness, and privacy issues that inform marketing strategy and digital policy design. Research populations include digital consumers, online systems and platforms, marketing audiences and behavior change contexts, and firms and institutions adapting to digital marketing and privacy norms.

Notable Publications:
- Influence of interactive features and personalization on consumer responses to online content
- Consumer privacy and tailored advertising - research on how privacy concerns alter data sharing and ad effectiveness
- Digital persuasive communication strategies influencing online behaviors

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Munson produces empirical insights on how digital information and marketing influence consumer decisions and privacy attitudes, informing organizational strategy and digital communication policy. Insights into consumer engagement and information framing could inform public communication campaigns about environmental topics including soil/nutrient management or water quality. His expertise in how people interact with digital information can be useful for crafting outreach or engagement strategies around environmental topics.
