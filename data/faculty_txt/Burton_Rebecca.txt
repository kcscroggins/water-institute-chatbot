Research / Program Focus:  
Rebecca Burton directs the Gulf Scholars Program at the University of Florida, equipping undergraduate students across sciences, engineering, medicine, humanities and arts with the skills and mindsets to address critical Gulf of Mexico region challenges: community health & resilience, environmental protection & stewardship, and offshore energy safety. Her work centres on interdisciplinary education, science communication, outreach, and experiential learning tied to coastal and Gulf-region systems.  

Professional Bio:  
Becca Burton brings more than a decade of journalistic, science‐communication, outreach and environmental program development experience. Before joining UF, she served as a founding member of the Thompson Earth Systems Institute and was communications coordinator for Florida Sea Grant. She also reported for major outlets (Health News Florida, The Gainesville Sun, The Miami Herald, and student journalism at The New York Times). A native of Pensacola, Florida, she holds a bachelor’s in journalism from Florida International University and a master’s in science & health communication from UF. At UF she teaches and mentors, builds community-engaged programs, and interfaces across the public service, environment and resilience spheres.
