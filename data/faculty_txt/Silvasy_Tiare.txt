Program Focus:
Tiare Silvasy is known for her work in applied residential horticulture and sustainable landscaping education, especially within the Florida-Friendly Landscaping program. She develops and delivers science-based Extension resources and educational programming to help homeowners design, install, and maintain landscapes that conserve water, protect soil, and reduce nutrient and pesticide runoff. Her outputs focus on environmental stewardship through ecologically sound home gardening and landscape practices. She employs development of Extension fact sheets and guides for homeowners, public workshops and community education events on Florida-Friendly landscaping, and collaboration with statewide Extension specialists and outreach partners. Program systems include residential and community landscapes in Florida, home gardeners and hobby growers and Master Gardener volunteers, and urban and suburban landowners interested in sustainable landscape stewardship.

Program Activities:
- Edible Landscaping Using the Nine Florida-Friendly Landscaping Principles - guidance on sustainable productive home gardens - EDIS
- Efficient Irrigation for Florida-Friendly Edible Landscapes - tips for water-wise irrigation and soil moisture management - EDIS
- Publications on pest management soil health mulching and wildlife coexistence
- Public workshops and community education events on sustainable landscaping

Extension Impact:
Her work focuses on translating horticultural and landscape science into behavior and practice that conserves water, protects soil and water quality, and reduces nutrient and pesticide inputs in residential settings, amplifying positive environmental outcomes through community adoption of best practices. She connects research-based horticultural principles to community action, helping residents incorporate sustainable practices that reduce environmental stressors.

Policy Relevance:
Tiare Silvasy's work supports environmental protection by empowering homeowners with science-based landscape and gardening practices that conserve water, protect soils, and reduce pollution in residential environments. Her work influences homeowner landscape stewardship, adoption of water conservation and soil protection practices, and implementation of Florida-Friendly principles in everyday landscapes.
