Research Focus:
Jamielyn Daugherty is a UF/IFAS Extension Agent serving Lake County, working at the interface of agriculture, natural resources, and community education. Her role centers on delivering UF/IFAS science to producers and residents to improve land management, water quality, and environmental stewardship. Her program areas include agricultural and natural resource outreach, water quality and nutrient stewardship, soil and land management practices, producer and community education, and adoption of UF/IFAS BMPs. She supports both agricultural producers and non-agricultural land managers in this mixed rural-exurban county.

Extension Programs:
- Agricultural and natural resource outreach
- Nutrient management education
- Soil health and land management practices
- Water quality and conservation programs
- BMP implementation support for producers
- Community environmental stewardship

Water & Policy Relevance:
- Reduced fertilizer misuse through education
- Improved soil management practices
- Better timing and placement of nutrients
- Protection of Lake County lakes, springs, and downstream waters
- Nonpoint source nutrient loading reduction
- Lake eutrophication risk reduction
- Soil organic matter maintenance
- Erosion and nutrient runoff reduction
- BMAP outreach at county level
