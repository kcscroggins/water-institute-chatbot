Program Focus:
Joseph Schaefer is known for his work in natural resources and wildlife extension, particularly urban wildlife issues, natural resource stewardship, and community-oriented conservation education. He served as a district extension director and has been linked with wildlife and range extension programming. He employs community education and workshops, extension publications and practical guidance on wildlife issues, and stakeholder engagement in natural resource topics. Program systems include Florida terrestrial and urban wildlife systems, natural resource stakeholders landowners and the general public.

Program Activities:
- Extension publications and outreach resources related to wildlife, natural resources, and stewardship
- Educational guides used in community programs
- Presentations and educational materials tied to wildlife habitat and community conservation topics

Extension Impact:
His work is practice-oriented and directed toward education, adoption of wildlife-friendly practices, and improving public understanding of natural resource issues, rather than academic research metrics. He bridges science and practice by delivering extension programming that helps communities understand and manage wildlife and natural resources responsibly.

Policy Relevance:
Joseph Schaefer's work supports sustainable wildlife and natural resource outcomes by equipping communities and stakeholders with science-based knowledge and practical strategies for coexistence and habitat management. His extension work plays a key role in translating ecological and wildlife science into actionable guidance for residents, landowners, and extension audiences, influencing how people interact with and manage natural systems. His work influences public understanding and adoption of wildlife stewardship practices, natural resource stewardship education and outreach, and practical application of ecological principles in community contexts.
