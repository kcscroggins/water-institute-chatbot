Program Focus:
Laura Tiu is known for her work in coastal and marine extension, with a focus on fisheries, aquaculture, seafood systems, and coastal community engagement. Her role centers on translating marine and fisheries science into practical guidance for stakeholders such as fishers, aquaculture producers, coastal managers, and seafood businesses. She emphasizes applied problem-solving, stakeholder education, and science-based decision support in coastal and working-waterfront contexts. She employs stakeholder engagement and participatory extension, development of extension publications workshops and training programs, synthesis and translation of fisheries and marine science for practitioners, and collaboration with researchers agencies and coastal communities. Program systems include Florida coastal and marine systems, commercial and recreational fisheries communities, aquaculture operations and seafood supply chains, and coastal counties and working waterfronts.

Program Activities:
- Florida Sea Grant extension fact sheets and guides related to fisheries aquaculture and seafood systems
- Workshops trainings and outreach programs for fishers aquaculture producers and coastal communities
- Collaborative reports and educational materials supporting sustainable fisheries and coastal livelihoods
- Impact measured through practice adoption stakeholder capacity-building and informed decision-making

Extension Impact:
Tiu's work supports evidence-based coastal and fisheries management by ensuring that scientific insights are accessible, usable, and actionable for practitioners and communities. She operates at a critical interface where science meets practice, helping ensure that fisheries and aquaculture knowledge actually informs decisions made by those who depend on coastal resources for their livelihoods. Her work influences adoption of sustainable fisheries and aquaculture practices, stakeholder understanding of marine science and policy, and capacity and resilience of coastal communities.
