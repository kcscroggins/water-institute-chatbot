Research Focus:
Dr. Serra is known for her work as an applied development economist with extensive research, teaching, and consultancy experience on African development issues, particularly in the Sahel region and francophone West Africa. Her expertise spans agricultural and livestock policy, political economy of reforms, gender and intersectionality in value chains, resilience to climate change, social capital, and rural transformation. She employs applied economic analysis using quantitative and qualitative methods, political economy frameworks for policy evaluation, gender and intersectional analytical approaches, and field-based and cross-country comparative studies. Research systems include West and East Africa including Sahelian countries such as Mali, Senegal, Niger, Chad, Mauritania, Ethiopia, Rwanda, and Uganda, rural and agricultural economies with emphasis on policy environments, and gendered economic participation and social sector reforms.

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Serra's work informs policies and interventions that strengthen agricultural and livestock sectors, advance gender equity in economic participation, and improve institutional frameworks for resilient development in West and East Africa. Her research directly addresses policy environments, institutional constraints, and socio-economic systems that shape agricultural productivity, rural livelihoods, and resilience to environmental and economic change in African economies, foundational for understanding development outcomes tied to environmental pressures and food systems. Her work influences agricultural policy and institutional reform, gender and intersectional economic participation, and development resilience and climate adaptation strategies.
