Research Focus:
Dr. Kalaman serves as the Florida-Friendly Landscaping™ Horticulture Agent for UF/IFAS Extension in Orange County. She focuses on sustainable landscaping practices including integrated pest management, soil and nutrient mitigation, water conservation, and pollinator-friendly plant programming. Her background includes research in pollinator-plant interactions, assessing how ornamental plant choices support bee and pollinator communities while promoting sustainable landscape design.

Water Institute Relevance:
Dr. Kalaman's work directly influences soil and water outcomes through sustainable landscaping adoption. Her Extension programming promotes reduced fertilizer use, appropriate plant selection, mulching, and water-wise landscaping that affect nutrient leaching and water quality. By promoting soil cover and plant diversity, she contributes to reduced runoff, improved infiltration, and healthier soil nutrient cycles in residential and urban landscapes—a critical component of decentralized nutrient management for protecting Florida's groundwater and surface waters.

Role in Research-to-Policy Pipeline:
Implementation and adoption layer. Bridges horticulture science and community adoption of sustainable land management, influencing behavior change in urban soil and water practices.

Strategic Value:
Answers the question: "How do residential and urban landscaping choices influence soil health and nutrient runoff?" Influences adoption of low-input, nutrient-smart landscaping and pollinator-friendly garden systems.
