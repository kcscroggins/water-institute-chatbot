Research Focus:
Dr. Kim is an Associate Professor and interdisciplinary geographer whose work centers on how recreation, parks, tourism, and community dynamics contribute to sustainable, healthy, and resilient societies. His research blends spatial analysis, community development, policy, and sustainability using geospatial technologies including GIS, GPS, LiDAR, and remote sensing. He studies planning, development, and management of parks, recreation, and tourism resources in coastal and inland recreational landscapes.

Notable Publications:
- Spatial studies of tourism and community sustainability in Annals of Tourism Research
- Geospatial modeling applications in tourism/recreation planning in Journal of Outdoor Recreation and Tourism
- Environmental and social dimensions of park and resource management using GIS and spatial econometrics
- Publications on tourism geographies and spatial analytics for social and environmental data
