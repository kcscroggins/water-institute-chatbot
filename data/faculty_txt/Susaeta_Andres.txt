Research Focus:
Dr. Susaeta is a forest economist whose work focuses on forest landowner decision-making, forest policy, timber markets, and the economics of forest management under uncertainty. He is especially known for analyzing how private forest owners respond to economic incentives, policy instruments, and risk, including markets for timber, bioenergy, ecosystem services, and carbon. His research bridges economic theory, applied modeling, and policy-relevant analysis in forest and natural resource systems. He employs econometric and statistical analysis, optimization and dynamic decision models, policy analysis and scenario modeling, survey-based and revealed-preference analysis of landowners, and integration of biophysical forest data with economic models. Research systems include Southern U.S. forest systems with strong emphasis on private non-industrial forest ownership landscapes and forest management contexts influenced by policy, markets, and climate risk.

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Susaeta's research informs forest and climate policy by quantifying how economic incentives and risk influence private landowner behavior, shaping forest management outcomes at landscape and regional scales. He addresses a core systems bottleneck: forests are largely managed by private actors, and environmental outcomes depend on how those actors respond to prices, policies, and risk. His work clarifies which incentives actually change behavior. His research influences economic and policy incentives for forest management, design of carbon biomass and ecosystem service markets, and understanding of private landowner responses to regulation and climate policy.
