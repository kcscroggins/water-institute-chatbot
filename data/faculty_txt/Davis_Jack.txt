Research Focus:
Jack Davis is one of the most influential environmental historians in the United States, best known for his work on American environmental history, conservation policy, and human-nature relationships, with a strong emphasis on Florida, the American South, and wetlands. His research areas include environmental history, conservation and land-use policy, wetlands, water, and coastal environments, race, power, and environmental governance, and long-run institutional drivers of ecological change. His work is foundational for understanding why today's water, soil, and nutrient problems exist.

Selected Publications:
- "An Everglades Providence: Marjory Stoneman Douglas and the American Environmental Century" (2009) - Pulitzer Prize winner; definitive history of Everglades conservation and water management politics
- "The Gulf: The Making of an American Sea" (2017) - Kirkus Prize winner; environmental history of oil, fisheries, wetlands, nutrient loading, and governance
- "Race, Class, and Environmental Politics in the Everglades" (Environmental History) - How power asymmetries shaped Everglades water management; ~300+ citations
