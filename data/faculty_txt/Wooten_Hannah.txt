Program Focus:
Hannah Wooten is an Extension professional focused on applied education, outreach, and technical assistance for agriculture and natural resource audiences. Her work emphasizes translating university research into practical guidance, supporting stakeholder understanding, and facilitating adoption of science-based practices at the community level. She employs workshops trainings and field days, development of Extension materials including fact sheets presentations and demonstrations, one-on-one consultations and group facilitation, and coordination with faculty agencies and local partners. Program systems include Florida communities served through UF/IFAS Extension, local agricultural and natural resource systems, and stakeholder groups including producers land managers and residents.

Program Activities:
- Extension fact sheets and educational resources
- Community workshops trainings and outreach programs
- Educational events supporting adoption of research-based practices
- Technical assistance for producers and land managers
- Impact measured through reach engagement and practice adoption

Extension Impact:
Wooten's work supports effective agricultural and environmental policy by ensuring research-based guidance is accessible, practical, and implemented by local stakeholders. She operates at the research-to-practice interface, helping convert scientific findings into informed decisions and on-the-ground action. Her work influences education and outreach effectiveness, stakeholder understanding and behavior change, and adoption of science-based management practices.
