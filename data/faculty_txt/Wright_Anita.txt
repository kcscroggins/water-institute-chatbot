Program Focus:
Anita Wright is known for applied food science and human nutrition education, with a focus on food safety, food quality, and science-based nutrition guidance. Her work emphasizes translating food science research into practical, consumer- and industry-facing education, supporting safe food handling, informed dietary choices, and effective food systems practices. She employs development and delivery of Extension education programs, creation of fact sheets guides and training materials, workshops certifications and community outreach, and collaboration with researchers regulators and industry partners. Program systems include consumers and community audiences, food service and food production stakeholders, and Florida food systems served through UF/IFAS Extension.

Program Activities:
- Food safety and nutrition fact sheets
- Training programs and workshops for food handlers
- Community and industry outreach initiatives
- Certification programs supporting food safety compliance
- Impact measured through reach compliance knowledge gain and practice adoption

Extension Impact:
Wright's work supports public health and food policy by ensuring that evidence-based food safety and nutrition guidance is accessible and effectively implemented. She plays a key role in translating food science into practice, supporting safer food systems and informed nutrition decisions at the community and industry level. Her work influences food safety compliance and education, consumer and industry understanding of nutrition and food handling, and adoption of science-based food system practices.
