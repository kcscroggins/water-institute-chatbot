Research Focus:
Eban Bean is an agricultural engineer specializing in water, nutrients, and agroecosystem sustainability, with a strong emphasis on applied, systems-level solutions. His research focuses on measuring, modeling, and reducing agricultural and urban impacts on water resources, especially in nutrient-sensitive systems. Key areas include nitrogen and phosphorus transport, agricultural runoff and leaching, edge-of-field and watershed-scale monitoring, Best Management Practices (BMPs) evaluation, modeling tools (SWAT, APEX, field-scale nutrient budgets), and climate variability and resilience in agrohydrologic systems. He produces the empirical evidence used to design and evaluate agricultural and urban BMPs and BMAP strategies for nutrient reduction and water-quality protection in Florida.
