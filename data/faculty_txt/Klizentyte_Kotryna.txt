Research Focus:
Dr. Klizentyte is an Assistant Professor specializing in economic valuation of ecosystem services and natural resource policy analysis. She uses stated and revealed preference models to assess public preferences and economic tradeoffs connected to environmental management decisions such as conservation programs, recreation, and land use change. Her research generates quantitative evidence on values and preferences that inform policy design and evaluation for natural resource management, especially where economic incentives and ecosystem services intersect.
