Research Focus:
Dr. Terrell T. "Red" Baker III is the Director and Professor of the School of Forest Resources and Conservation (SFRC) at the University of Florida. He leads the interdisciplinary academic and outreach mission across forestry, fisheries, and geomatics sciences, serving faculty, staff, and students across undergraduate, graduate, and extension programs. His research expertise spans forest ecology, streamside management zones, watershed processes, fire ecology, and natural resource conflict mediation. Before joining UF, Dr. Baker served as Chair of the Department of Forestry at the University of Kentucky, where he was the James Graham Brown Endowed Professor of Forestry. He also spent many years as a Riparian (Streamside) Specialist at New Mexico State University, balancing research and extension in riverine and forest ecosystems.
