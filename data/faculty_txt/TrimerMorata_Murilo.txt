Program Focus:
Murilo Trimer Morata is known for applied extension work at the intersection of agriculture, natural resources, and community engagement, with an emphasis on translating research into practical guidance for producers, land managers, and local stakeholders. His profile reflects hands-on program delivery, stakeholder training, and outreach, rather than a research-only focus. He employs development and delivery of workshops trainings and demonstrations, one-on-one technical assistance and stakeholder advising, creation of extension materials including presentations guides and outreach content, and coordination with researchers agencies and community partners. Program systems include UF/IFAS Extension service areas, agricultural producers and land managers, and community and institutional stakeholders interacting with agricultural and natural resource systems.

Extension Impact:
Morata's work supports effective implementation of science-based practices by connecting research institutions with producers and communities, improving uptake and on-the-ground outcomes. He operates at a critical translation layer, ensuring that research and institutional knowledge actually reach and benefit end users, which is often the limiting step in achieving environmental and agricultural goals. His work influences practice adoption and stakeholder behavior, effectiveness of extension and outreach programs, and community engagement with agricultural and natural resource science.
