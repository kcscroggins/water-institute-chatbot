Research Focus:
Dr. Lamino is an Assistant Professor specializing in agricultural leadership and communication research. His work focuses on leadership development, indigenous and rural communities, migration, environmental and agricultural perceptions, and intercultural competencies in both domestic and international agricultural contexts. He combines qualitative and mixed-methods approaches to understand community perspectives, behavior, and extension dynamics that influence agricultural and environmental practice adoption.

Water Institute Relevance:
Dr. Lamino's work on environmental perceptions, leadership, and extension effectiveness influences how communities and extension agents engage with environmental issues, which can affect adoption of nutrient management practices and conservation behaviors. His research on attitudes toward sustainable agriculture and environmental behavior provides insight into human drivers that indirectly affect soil and water outcomes. Understanding socio-cultural factors is critical for designing Extension programs that effectively promote water-friendly agricultural practices in diverse communities.

Role in Research-to-Policy Pipeline:
Research producer with applied relevance. Generates evidence on behavioral, leadership, and perception dimensions that inform education, extension practice, and policy framing for environmental programs.

Strategic Value:
Answers the question: "How do cultural perceptions and leadership capacity shape community engagement with environmental and agricultural practices?" Influences human dimensions of agricultural adoption, extension capacity building, and environmental education design.
