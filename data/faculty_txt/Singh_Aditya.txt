Research Focus:
Dr. Singh is known for his application of optical remote sensing and spectroscopy to ecosystem science and precision agriculture, especially in assessing plant nutrient status, plant health, water and stress responses in vegetation, and landscape function. His work integrates leaf-level, proximal canopy, airborne, and satellite spectral data to infer plant and ecosystem processes, advancing mechanistic models of ecosystem function that span agricultural and natural systems. He employs multi-scale optical reflectance spectroscopy, proximal and airborne imaging spectroscopy, integration of remote sensing with mechanistic process models, and development of remote sensing-derived products for stakeholders. Research systems include forest and grassland ecosystem structure and function, agricultural ecosystems with optical stress detection for irrigation demand and nutrient use, and landscape-scale nutrient and water stress assessment.

Notable Publications:
- Imaging spectroscopy methods for mapping canopy foliar chemical and morphological traits
- Applications of Unmanned Aerial Systems in Agricultural Operation Management multi-part extension series on platforms payloads and best practices
- Co-authorship on remote sensing and artificial intelligence applications for crop yield forecasting and sensing workflows - extension publications
- Peer-reviewed research on imaging spectroscopy algorithms and canopy traits

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Singh's research advances remote sensing tools that support environmental monitoring, precision agriculture decision support, and ecosystem process understanding, improving capacity to detect and manage plant stress and resource use at scale. His remote sensing techniques provide critical observational data for understanding vegetation water and nutrient stress, plant functioning, and ecosystem responses to environmental change. This enables quantitative assessment of stress drivers, nutrient status, and functional dynamics across scales. His work influences remote sensing data integration for ecosystem health, plant stress and nutrient status detection, and development of decision-support products based on spectral analysis.
