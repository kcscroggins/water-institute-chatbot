Research Focus:
Dr. Wilson is an environmental engineer known for his work on water quality, contaminant fate and transport, and treatment technologies in agricultural and urban water systems. His research focuses on how nutrients and contaminants move through soils and waters, and how engineered and nature-based solutions can reduce pollution and improve water quality, particularly in Florida's coastal and agricultural regions. He employs field and laboratory measurements of water and soil quality, design and evaluation of treatment wetlands and best management practices, nutrient mass balance and transport analysis, monitoring of surface and groundwater systems, and applied engineering analysis for real-world systems. Research systems include Florida agricultural landscapes especially south and coastal Florida, watersheds connected to the Indian River Lagoon and estuarine systems, and managed treatment systems bridging land and water.

Notable Publications:
- Nutrient removal efficiency of treatment wetlands
- Fate and transport of nitrogen and phosphorus
- Performance of engineered systems under field conditions
- Design principles for water-quality improvement in agricultural regions
- Publications in environmental engineering and water-quality journals
- Applied reports BMP evaluations and stakeholder-facing guidance for Florida water management

Policy Relevance:
Wilson's work provides the technical basis for water-quality regulations, BMP design, and infrastructure investments aimed at reducing nutrient pollution. He contributes actionable engineering science that directly informs how nutrient pollution is reduced in real landscapes, especially in environmentally sensitive coastal regions. His work influences design and performance of nutrient mitigation infrastructure, water-quality compliance and regulatory strategy, and implementation of BMPs and treatment systems.
