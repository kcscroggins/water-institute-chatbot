Research Focus:
Michael D'Imperio is an Extension professional focused on community outreach, environmental education, and local natural resource issues in Sarasota County. His work centers on translating UF/IFAS science into actionable practices for residents, local governments, and community groups. His program areas include environmental education and outreach, residential landscapes and sustainability, water quality awareness and protection, community programs tied to natural resources, and local Extension programming through workshops, talks, and public guidance.

Extension Programs:
- Florida-Friendly Landscaping education and outreach
- Residential water conservation programs
- Environmental stewardship workshops
- Community engagement on natural resource issues
- Public guidance on landscape best practices

Water & Policy Relevance:
- Local adoption of practices that protect water quality

- Awareness of nutrient runoff to Sarasota Bay
- Urban nutrient loading reduction
- Stormwater quality improvement
- BMAP outreach support at county level
- Last-mile delivery of UF/IFAS research to communities
