Professional Role:
Tonya Ashworth is an Environmental Horticulture Agent II for UF/IFAS Extension Duval County, based in Jacksonville, Florida. Her work focuses on translating university research into practical knowledge for Florida residents, supporting community and environmental initiatives, and coordinating Extension programming for stakeholders across Northeast Florida.

Focus Areas:
- Extension education and outreach
- Environmental horticulture
- Florida-friendly landscaping and gardening
- Invasive plant species management and biological control
- Community engagement and knowledge transfer
- Program coordination and implementation

Programs & Coordination:
- Master Gardener Volunteer Training Program Coordinator (annual training in August)
- Community horticultural education
- Stakeholder training and workshops

Extension Publications & Outreach:
- Air potato beetle biological control release programs
- Tomato gardening and seed starting guides
- Sterile plant varieties for invasive species management
- Edible weeds identification for Florida backyards
- Herb gardening guidance for Florida conditions
- Advice for new Florida residents on gardening adaptation

Impact & Role Context:
As Extension faculty, Ashworth's impact is measured through program leadership, community engagement, and applied outputs rather than peer-reviewed publications. She serves as a "last-mile" connection between UF research and Florida communities, ideal for translational projects, community-facing initiatives, and education/outreach deployments.
