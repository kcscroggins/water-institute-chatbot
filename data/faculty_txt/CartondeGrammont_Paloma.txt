Research Focus:
Dr. Carton de Grammont is an environmental geographer and trained facilitator who designs and guides processes that allow groups to advance their ideas and reach agreement by encouraging full and equitable participation. With over 15 years of experience, she specializes in coordinating interdisciplinary, multi-institutional and international teams that conduct research and develop policies in diverse topics of natural resource management and environmental change. Her career spans academic institutions, government agencies, and nonprofit organizations in both the United States and Mexico.

In her capacity as Assistant Director, she plays a pivotal role in spearheading the Water Institute's Outreach, Stakeholder Engagement, and Communication initiatives. She leverages her interdisciplinary background, extensive professional experience, and facilitation skills to support the Water Institute's Research, Education, and Outreach endeavors.

Professional Bio:
Dr. Carton de Grammont earned her Bachelor's degree in Biology, Master's degree in Environmental Sciences, and Doctorate in Geography, all from Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México. She is also a program alumna of UF's Natural Resources Leadership Institute. She joined the UF Water Institute in September 2018 as Research Coordinator before becoming Assistant Director. Her multilingual proficiency (Spanish and French) combined with her Latin American experience positions her to advance the Institute's international research agenda.

Notable Projects & Contributions:
- Coordinated funding for ProForest, a regional forest health initiative involving multiple institutions
- Contributed to developing comprehensive Biodiversity Conservation Strategies for Lake Erie and Lake Michigan
- Led research efforts to identify and protect endangered species in Mexico
- Coordinated the update of Mexico's national red list for endangered species
- Served as a consultant with CONABIO, leading the academic subcommittee for an International Expert Workshop on CITES Non-Detriment Findings held in Cancún, Mexico
- Orchestrated the design of the "Biodiversity Conservation and Sustainable Management Strategy for the State of Michoacán, Mexico"
- Part of a team awarded a $2.5 million grant from the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers to support research on harmful algal blooms in Lake Okeechobee and related watersheds
- Facilitation Leader for the Carbonate Critical Zone Research Coordination Network

Notable Publications (selected):

- Research on endemic species conservation in Cozumel Island, Mexico, including critically endangered Pygmy Raccoon (Procyon pygmaeus) and Dwarf Coati (Nasua nelsoni)
