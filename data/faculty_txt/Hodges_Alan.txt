Research Focus:
Alan Hodges is an applied environmental economist specializing in economic impact and cost-benefit analysis of agriculture, conservation, and natural-resource systems. His research areas include agricultural and environmental economics, economic impact analysis (IMPLAN and related tools), cost-benefit analysis of conservation and resource programs, valuation of ecosystem services, and regional and sectoral economic modeling. His work provides economic evidence that informs policy justification, program evaluation, funding decisions, and regulatory trade-offs for Florida's agricultural and natural-resource sectors.

Selected Publications:
- Economic impact studies of Florida agriculture, forestry, fisheries, and natural-resource sectors
- Analyses supporting water management districts, conservation programs, and land-use policy decisions
- UF/IFAS reports quantifying jobs, income, and value-added effects of environmental and agricultural systems

Water & Policy Relevance:
- Economic costs, benefits, and trade-offs of soil conservation programs
- Cost-benefit analysis of nutrient management and water quality programs
- Economic incentives influencing nutrient-reduction practice adoption
- Funding justification for conservation and restoration programs
- Policy design and legislative support for environmental programs
- Regional economic effects of water management decisions
- Economic viability assessment for BMPs and conservation practices
- Stakeholder buy-in through demonstrated economic benefits
