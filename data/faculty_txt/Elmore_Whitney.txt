Research Focus:
Whitney Elmore is a UF/IFAS Extension Agent whose work centers on community education and applied outreach around agriculture, natural resources, and environmental stewardship. Her role focuses on helping residents, producers, and local partners adopt UF/IFAS best management practices (BMPs). Her program areas include nutrient stewardship and fertilizer education, soil health and land management, water conservation and protection, residential and small-scale agricultural practices, and UF/IFAS BMP dissemination.

Extension Programs:
- Nutrient management and fertilizer education
- Soil health and land management practices
- Water conservation and protection programs
- Residential and small-scale agricultural outreach
- BMP adoption support for producers and residents
- Environmental stewardship education

Water & Policy Relevance:
- Improved fertilizer timing and application rates
- Reduced over-application of nutrients
- Better soil cover and erosion prevention
- Awareness of nutrient runoff impacts
- Nonpoint source nutrient loading reduction
- Soil nutrient retention improvement
- Downstream water quality protection
- BMAP outreach at community level
- Last-mile delivery of UF/IFAS research to stakeholders
