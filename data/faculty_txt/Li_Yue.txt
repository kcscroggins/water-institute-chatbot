Research Focus:
Dr. Li is known for her work in environmental education theory and practice, focusing on how educators learn, change behavior, and engage communities around environmental and sustainability issues, including the innovative use of technology (such as AI) to enhance environmental learning and professional development for environmental educators. Her research examines sense of place, transformative learning, behavior change, and professional learning networks for environmental educators in formal and informal settings.

Notable Publications:
- Interactive learning in an urban environmental education online course - explores educational design and outcomes in online environmental learning contexts
- Practice change in environmental education: lessons from professional development - investigates how professional development affects practice change in environmental educators
- Relationship between professional networks and practice change in environmental education (The Journal of Environmental Education) - examines how professional network development influences educators' practices
- Additional work on environmental educator learning communities, network effects on EE practice, and evidence-based strategies for engaging learners in sustainability contexts

Water Institute Relevance:
Dr. Li's work on environmental education supports shifts in knowledge, attitudes, and behaviors that underlie environmental stewardship and community engagement, which can affect how communities understand and act on issues like water quality, ecosystem health, and sustainable land/water practices. Environmental education is fundamental to motivating stewardship of natural systems including soil and water. Her contributions inform how people learn about and engage with environmental issues, building capacity for effective community engagement in conservation and environmental quality protection.
