Research Focus:
Lisa Leslie is a Family and Consumer Sciences Extension Agent focused on personal finance education, financial literacy coaching, and community outreach programs. She develops and delivers educational content and services aimed at improving financial decision-making and household economic stability. Her work includes workshops, webinars, mentoring, and educational resources on budgeting, debt management, savings, credit scores, tax deductions, and estate planning for residents and families in Hillsborough County and broader Florida.

Notable Publications and Outputs:
- Extension blog posts and newsletters on personal finance topics (tax deductions, estate planning, credit scores)
- Personal finance newsletters and educational resources for Hillsborough County residents
- "YOU and YOUR CREDIT: Credit Scores" - co-authored fact sheet on credit and financial literacy
- Community workshops and mentoring programs on money management

Extension Programs:
- Personal finance education workshops and webinars

- Community outreach on household economic decision-making

Water Institute Relevance:
While Leslie's expertise is in human dimensions and financial education rather than environmental sciences, her role in family and community education contributes to community resilience and economic stability. Extension programs that bolster economic security can indirectly affect household choices about resource use, housing decisions, and community engagement with environmental programs. Community economic capacity is a component of broader sustainability initiatives that intersect with resource management challenges.

Role in Research-to-Policy Pipeline:
Extension/implementation specialist. Translates research-based financial management and family science knowledge into community education, workshops, mentoring, and outreach, supporting individual and family economic wellbeing and community readiness for sustainability initiatives.

Strategic Value:
Answers the question: "How can Extension education improve community economic capacity and decision-making?" Influences financial literacy and budgeting behavior, adoption of informed economic practices, and community engagement in Extension programming that supports broader resource stewardship.
