Research Focus:
Ron Chandler is an Associate Instructional Professor whose work bridges psychology, sustainability, and environmental decision making. His unique background spans ecology, hydrology, and psychology, from early work in water quality monitoring and lake/stream ecology to leadership of conservation initiatives and eventual doctoral study in psychology. He develops and teaches Sustainability Psychology, an emerging field that uses psychological theory to improve the effectiveness of sustainability education and programs. His interests include human dignity, prosocial behavior, and environmental identity as drivers of sustainable action, as well as how cultural and social factors influence environmental decisions and behaviors. He founded the Conservation Initiative for the Asian Elephant, focusing on holistic, cross-cultural approaches to human-wildlife coexistence.
