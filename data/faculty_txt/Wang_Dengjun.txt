Research Focus:
Dr. Wang's research encompasses conventional and emerging contaminants in agricultural systems. Primary interests include PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) transformation and remediation, nutrient-related water quality issues and mitigation in agriculture, nanotechnology applications for pesticide removal and sensing, sustainable agriculture using nanofertilizers, and microplastics/nanoplastics fate and transport. His methodology includes field sampling, laboratory experiments, and mathematical modeling.

Notable Projects:
- DoD SERDP projects on PFAS transformation and remediation
- USEPA-funded nanotechnology research for pesticide removal
- USDA-NIFA project on nutrients, water quality, and harmful algal blooms
