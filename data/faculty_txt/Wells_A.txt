Program Focus:
A. Ondine Wells is known for applied Extension work focused on translating environmental, agricultural, and natural resource science into actionable guidance for local communities. Her work emphasizes education, outreach, and stakeholder engagement, supporting informed decision-making and adoption of best practices in land, water, and resource stewardship. She employs design and delivery of workshops trainings and public programs, development of Extension publications and educational materials, one-on-one technical assistance and group facilitation, and collaboration with researchers agencies and community partners. Program systems include Florida communities served through UF/IFAS Extension, local and regional agricultural and natural resource contexts, and extension audiences including landowners producers and community members.

Program Activities:
- Extension fact sheets and educational resources
- Community workshops trainings and outreach events
- Programmatic materials supporting adoption of science-based practices
- Applied and collaborative contributions aligned with Extension goals
- Impact measured through knowledge transfer participation and behavior change

Extension Impact:
Wells's work supports effective environmental and agricultural policy by ensuring research-based guidance is accessible and actionable for local stakeholders and decision-makers. She operates at a critical interface between research and practice, helping ensure that evidence-based recommendations are understood and implemented within communities. Her work influences education and outreach effectiveness, stakeholder engagement and understanding, and adoption of science-based stewardship practices.
