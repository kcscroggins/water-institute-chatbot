Research Focus:
Dr. Lang is a Research Associate at the Everglades Research and Education Center specializing in water quality and soil fertility research in the Everglades Agricultural Area (EAA). His work focuses on phosphorus dynamics, sediment-associated nutrient transport, and best management practices (BMPs) designed to reduce nutrient export from agricultural lands to downstream ecosystems. He quantifies biogeophysical controls on phosphorus transport including sediment adsorption/desorption, vegetation interactions, and hydrologic dynamics.

Notable Publications and Outputs:
- Best Management Practices in the Everglades Agricultural Area: Controlling Particulate Phosphorus and Canal Sediments (IFAS EDIS)
- Environmental and management factors influencing drainage water P loads from EAA farms
- Phosphorus sorption and sediment assessment in Everglades A.A. canals - documenting sediments as sinks and sources of phosphorus
- Implementation and verification of BMPs for reducing P loading in Everglades Agricultural Area
- IFAS Extension fact sheets on biochar production, sugarcane nutrient diagnostics, and environmental practice guides
