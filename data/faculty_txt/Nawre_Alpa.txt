Research Focus:
Dr. Nawre is a landscape architect and urban designer whose scholarship and practice focus on water landscapes, resilient low-cost water infrastructure, and socio-culturally integrated design solutions â€” particularly by drawing on vernacular water management practices from the Indian subcontinent and other contexts to inform sustainable landscape architecture and urban design. Policy Relevance:
Dr. Nawre produces scholarship and design practice that inform resilient water-sensitive landscape design and infrastructure planning, contributing to broader dialogues on sustainable water management and equitable landscape interventions. Landscape design that emphasizes resilient water infrastructure may influence stormwater management, runoff reduction, and green infrastructure planning, which are relevant to nutrient transport and water quality outcomes in built environments. Her community-engaged design projects and scholarship inform spatial planning and landscape policy discussions around integrating water infrastructure with social resilience.
