Research Focus:
Luke Harlow is a UF/IFAS Extension professional focused on helping producers and land managers implement science-based agricultural and natural-resource practices. His program areas include Extension education and outreach, agricultural and natural-resource best management practices, producer training and technical assistance, and local-scale problem solving and stakeholder engagement. He operates at the point where research and policy are operationalized, focusing on behavior change, technical assistance, and local capacity-building.

Extension Programs:
- UF/IFAS Extension fact sheets and guidance materials
- Workshops, field days, and trainings on BMPs
- One-on-one technical assistance with producers and landowners
- Local problem-solving for agricultural and environmental challenges
- Stakeholder engagement and capacity-building
- Coordination between research institutions and land managers

Water & Policy Relevance:
- Implementation of soil-health and nutrient-management practices at farm level
- Practice adoption affecting nutrient loss, runoff, and water quality
- BMP and conservation practice adoption support
- Local compliance with nutrient and water regulations
- Building producer trust and engagement with science
- Removing barriers to soil and nutrient practice implementation
- Last-mile delivery of research to practitioners
- Ensuring policies translate into real-world outcomes
