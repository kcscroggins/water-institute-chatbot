Research Focus:
James Cuda is a nationally recognized biological control entomologist whose career focused on the classical biological control of invasive plants, especially using host-specific insects to suppress invasive weeds in Florida and the southeastern U.S. His research areas include classical biological control of invasive plants, insect-weed interactions, host specificity and risk assessment, integrated pest and weed management, and restoration of invaded ecosystems. His work is foundational for long-term, low-input invasive plant management, especially in wetlands and natural areas.
