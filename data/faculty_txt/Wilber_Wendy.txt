Program Focus:
Wendy Wilber is known for applied Extension programming and community education focused on agriculture, natural resources, and environmental stewardship. Her work centers on translating research-based information into practical guidance for local stakeholders, supporting informed decision-making and adoption of best practices. She employs design and facilitation of workshops trainings and educational events, development of Extension materials including fact sheets presentations and demonstrations, one-on-one technical assistance and group facilitation, and collaboration with researchers agencies and community partners. Program systems include Florida communities served through UF/IFAS Extension, local and regional agricultural and natural resource systems, and extension audiences including landowners producers and community members.

Program Activities:
- Extension fact sheets and educational resources
- Community workshops trainings and outreach programs
- Educational events supporting adoption of science-based practices
- Technical assistance for landowners and producers
- Impact measured through participation knowledge transfer and practice adoption

Extension Impact:
Wilber's work supports effective agricultural and environmental policy by ensuring research-based guidance is accessible, practical, and actionable for local stakeholders. She operates at the critical interface between research and practice, helping ensure that science-based recommendations are understood and applied within communities. Her work influences education and outreach effectiveness, stakeholder understanding and engagement, and adoption of science-based stewardship practices.
