Research Focus:
Dr. Yang investigates human-environment interactions through quantitative analysis. His work bridges operational uses of scientific remote sensing and GIS techniques and land management practices. Current priorities include NEON science, geospatial modeling, citizen science, and Volunteered Geographic Information applications at macrosystems scales.

Notable Projects:
- EMERGE: A participatory science curriculum for mapping mosquitoes and land cover (2025)
- NASA Grant: "Forecasting Mosquito-Borne Disease Risk in a Changing Climate"
- NEON data studies mapping dead tree clusters and wildfire vulnerability in Yellowstone
- UF Global Fellows Program research across Ecuador, Guatemala, and Mexico
