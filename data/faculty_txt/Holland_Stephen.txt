Research Focus:
Stephen Holland is a tourism and recreation scholar specializing in outdoor recreation and the social and economic value of natural landscapes. His research areas include outdoor recreation and nature-based tourism, park and protected-area management, visitor behavior and experience, economic and social value of natural resources, and sustainable tourism and recreation planning. His work connects environmental conditions to recreation demand, tourism value, and community outcomes, informing management and policy decisions for natural areas.

Selected Publications:
- Peer-reviewed research on recreation demand, visitor satisfaction, and management strategies in natural settings
- Studies linking environmental quality and access to tourism and recreation outcomes
- Applied work supporting park planning, visitor management, and sustainability frameworks
