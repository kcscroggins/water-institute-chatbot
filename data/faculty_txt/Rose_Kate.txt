Program Focus:
Kate Rose is known for her work as a marine science educator and extension agent, translating coastal and marine research into community-focused education, stewardship, and citizen science programs. She engages stakeholders in coastal and estuarine science, promotes environmental stewardship, and leads programming that connects local communities with marine conservation issues. She employs extension programming and workshops, citizen science coordination such as seagrass monitoring, public engagement and informal education, and collaboration with scientists volunteers and local stakeholders. Program systems include coastal ecosystems and estuaries particularly the Charlotte Harbor area, local residents volunteers and community groups, and marine and coastal educational audiences.

Program Activities:
- Extension articles and blog posts on coastal and marine science topics including water quality issues, estuarine changes, and community science opportunities
- Leadership and facilitation of programs such as Eyes on Seagrass which engages volunteers in monitoring seagrass health, water conditions, and related coastal indicators
- Organization and instruction for Florida Master Naturalist Program courses focusing on coastal systems and hands-on ecological learning

Extension Impact:
Her outputs emphasize education, environmental stewardship, and participatory science rather than traditional academic publications. She advances public understanding and community action around coastal science and resource conservation, helping bridge the gap between research, policy, and on-the-ground stewardship.

Policy Relevance:
Kate Rose's work supports sustainable coastal management by empowering local communities and stakeholders with science-based knowledge, citizen science participation, and environmental engagement. Her work directly connects scientific understanding of coastal and estuarine ecosystems with local community awareness, participation, and resource stewardship - a key function for translating research into practice and policy-aware community action. She influences science communication and environmental education, citizen science and stakeholder participation, and community stewardship of coastal ecosystems.
