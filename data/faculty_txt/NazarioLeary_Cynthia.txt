Research Focus:
Dr. Nazario-Leary is an environmental horticulture specialist and Extension educator focused on science-based home and community landscaping practices that improve plant health, enhance biodiversity (including pollinators), and support environmentally friendly landscape design. She leads outreach, workshops, and community programs on sustainable horticulture and gardening. Her work translates scientific horticultural knowledge into outreach, training, and community education that empowers residents and landscape professionals to adopt practices that support healthy soils, water conservation, and plant-environment interactions. Research populations include home and community landscapes in Alachua County, Florida, gardeners, landscape professionals, and the public.

Program Activities:
- Workshops and community programs on fall gardening, vegetable planting, and sustainable landscape design
- Leadership in Florida-Friendly Landscaping and Master Gardener educational offerings
- Public talks and plant health education on topics such as common plant diseases, pollinators, and landscape best practices
- Practical guidance and interactive learning experiences helping residents apply soil, water, and plant science

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Nazario-Leary delivers science-based education on landscape practices that enhance soil health, support water conservation, and minimize excess nutrient inputs in residential and community settings. Her Florida-Friendly Landscaping education promotes practices that influence soil health, reduced fertilizer use, and improved water retention, all relevant to nutrient dynamics in residential landscapes. Through outreach on sustainable gardening, pollinators, and yard care, her programming can reduce excess nutrient inputs and support water-conserving practices in home landscapes, indirectly reducing nutrient runoff concerns.
