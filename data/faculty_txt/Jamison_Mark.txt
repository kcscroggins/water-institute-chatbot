Research Focus:
Dr. Jamison is a Professor and Director of the Public Utilities Research Center (PURC) at the University of Florida. He is internationally recognized for his expertise in regulation and economics of public utilities, especially water, energy, telecommunications, and infrastructure governance. His work focuses on utility regulation, market design, institutional reform, and regulatory capacity-building, with particular emphasis on developing and transition economies. He addresses critical questions about how utilities are financed, regulated, and incentivized to invest in infrastructure improvements.

Water Institute Relevance:
Dr. Jamison's expertise directly impacts how water and wastewater utilities are financed, regulated, and incentivized. His work addresses why utilities may or may not invest in nutrient removal and treatment technologies, even when scientific evidence supports such investments. Utility regulatory decisions he studies shape nutrient discharges to rivers, springs, estuaries, and coasts throughout Florida.
