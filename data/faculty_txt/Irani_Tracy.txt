Research Focus:
Tracy Irani is a public issues education scholar specializing in deliberative engagement and science communication for contentious environmental and agricultural policies. Her research areas include public issues education and deliberative engagement, science communication and public understanding, risk, trust, and values in contentious policy domains, stakeholder engagement and facilitated dialogue, and design of participatory decision processes. Her work shapes how evidence enters public discourse, policy debates, and collective decision-making, particularly for controversial science-based issues in agriculture, environment, biotechnology, water, and sustainability.

Selected Publications:
- Peer-reviewed research on public engagement, framing, and deliberation around science-based public issues
- Public Issues Education programs and toolkits training facilitators to conduct evidence-informed dialogue
- Applied projects supporting policy conversations on agriculture, environment, and natural resources

Water & Policy Relevance:
- How soil health, nutrient management, and water quality science enters public and policy arenas
- Public engagement influencing policy adoption and compliance
- Long-term stewardship behaviors affected by deliberation quality
- Public trust and legitimacy for environmental policies
- Policy durability and social license for nutrient regulations
- Stakeholder engagement quality improvement
- Integrating science into public deliberation for evidence-based yet legitimate policies
- Addressing governance bottlenecks from conflict and mistrust
