Research Focus:
Dr. Nino is a leading materials scientist known for his work on electrochemical energy storage (especially supercapacitors and batteries), polymer electrolytes, nanostructured materials, interfacial phenomena, and materials characterization. His research integrates materials chemistry, physics, and engineering to solve challenges in energy storage and conversion. Research systems include electrochemical devices and materials systems, solid and soft matter electrolytes, and nanostructured conductive and dielectric materials. His extensive publication record appears in high-impact journals including Advanced Materials, Journal of Materials Chemistry, Electrochimica Acta, and ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces.

Notable Publications:
- Advanced supercapacitor materials with high capacitance and stability
- Polymer electrolytes with enhanced ionic conductivity
- Nanostructured composites for energy storage applications
- Interfacial charge transport in electrochemical devices
- Materials design strategies for improved energy density and cycling performance

Policy Relevance:
Dr. Nino produces foundational evidence on advanced materials for energy storage and conversion, contributing to technologies that support sustainable energy systems, with indirect implications for environmental resource strategies. Energy storage technologies can influence broader environmental impacts, including how renewable systems affect land and water use. His work supports transitions to low-carbon energy sources that may reduce environmental pressures on water and land resources.
